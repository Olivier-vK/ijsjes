About

This website is a fanpage about the Anser Anser

Geese are waterfowl belonging to the family Anatidae. They are closely related to ducks and swans, and are known for their distinctive honking calls, long necks, and webbed feet. Geese are found in various habitats around the world, including wetlands, lakes, rivers, and grasslands.

Geese are herbivorous birds, primarily feeding on grasses, grains, and aquatic plants. They are known for their migratory behavior, often flying in V-shaped formations during long-distance migrations to take advantage of aerodynamic efficiency.

Geese are social birds that often form large flocks, especially during migration

Interesting Facts

  • Geese are known for their strong family bonds and often mate for life.
  • They have a unique way of communicating through honking sounds, which can vary in pitch and intensity.
  • Geese are excellent swimmers and can also walk on land with ease due to their webbed feet.
  • During migration, geese can fly at altitudes of up to 29,000 feet (8,800 meters) and cover distances of over 3,000 miles (4,800 kilometers).
  • Some species of geese are known to be aggressive when defending their nests or young, often hissing and flapping their wings to ward off intruders.
