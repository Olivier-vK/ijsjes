Greylag Goose (Grauwe Gans)

The Greylag Goose (Anser anser) is the ancestor of most domestic geese. It is native to Europe and Asia, and is known for its grey-brown plumage and orange beak. These geese are found in wetlands, lakes, and rivers throughout their range.

Greylag geese are large birds, typically weighing between 2.5 to 4.1 kg. They are herbivorous and feed on grasses, grains, and aquatic plants. They form strong pair bonds and often mate for life.

Canada Goose (Canadese Gans)

The Canada Goose (Branta canadensis) is one of the most recognizable geese species, known for its distinctive black head and neck with white cheek patches. Originally native to North America, they have been introduced to many other parts of the world.

Canada geese are highly adaptable and can be found in a variety of habitats including parks, golf courses, and urban areas. They are known for their loud honking calls and V-shaped flight formations during migration.

Snow Goose (Sneeuwgans)

The Snow Goose (Anser caerulescens) is known for its white plumage, though some individuals have a dark "blue" morph. They breed in the Arctic and migrate long distances to wintering grounds in the southern United States and Mexico.

Snow geese form large flocks during migration, sometimes numbering in the tens of thousands. They feed primarily on grasses, sedges, and grains in agricultural fields.

Barnacle Goose (Brandgans)

The Barnacle Goose (Branta leucopsis) is a distinctive goose with a black head, neck, and breast, white face, and grey body. They breed in the Arctic regions of Greenland, Svalbard, and Russia, and winter in northwestern Europe.

These geese are named after the barnacle myth, where people once believed they hatched from barnacles. They are social birds that nest in colonies on cliffs and rocky outcrops.

Egyptian Goose (Nijlgans)

The Egyptian Goose (Alopochen aegyptiaca) is native to Africa but has been introduced to Europe and other regions. They have distinctive brown and white plumage with a dark eye patch and pink legs.

Egyptian geese are often found near water bodies and are known for their aggressive behavior, especially during breeding season. They are monogamous and form strong pair bonds.

Brent Goose (Rotgans)

The Brent Goose (Branta bernicla) is a small, dark goose with a black head, neck, and breast. They breed in the high Arctic and migrate to coastal areas in Europe, Asia, and North America for the winter.

Brent geese are primarily marine birds, feeding on eelgrass and other marine vegetation. They are known for their long-distance migrations and tight family bonds.
